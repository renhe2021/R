# Chapter 1: PE Ratio and Value Investing

The Price-to-Earnings (PE) ratio is one of the most important metrics in value investing. A PE ratio below 15 generally indicates an undervalued stock. When combined with a Return on Equity (ROE) above 15 percent, it signals a high-quality company trading at a discount.
Warren Buffett often looks for companies with consistent earnings growth and reasonable PE ratios. The ideal stock screening rule is: PE < 15 AND ROE > 15% AND Debt-to-Equity < 0.5. This ensures the company is both profitable and financially stable.
Price-to-Book (PB) ratio below 1.5 is another important signal. Benjamin Graham suggested buying stocks when PB < 1.0 for maximum safety margin.
